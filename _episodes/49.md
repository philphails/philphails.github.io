# Simple, practical ways to understand your digestion and feel better in your body and mind

*Phillip Jones sits down with functional nutritionist Ashley Eaves to unpack gut health — from daily fiber and probiotics to signs of imbalance and her practical 2-week detox protocol. Real talk on digestion, energy, and feeling better.*

![Ashley Eaves Returns](../assets/images/ashleyEavesReturns.jpg)

Ashley Eaves and I had a long, practical conversation about gut health. We covered what a healthy gut actually needs, the common signs that yours might be off, and straightforward ways to support it without gimmicks.

The gut influences so much — energy, mood, skin, immunity, even how clearly we think. Ashley’s approach is grounded and actionable, blending real food, awareness, and targeted support.

## Why Fiber Matters More Than Most People Realize

Fiber creates bulk and feeds healthy bacteria. Most people need 25–30 grams daily from fruits, vegetables, whole grains, nuts, and seeds. It helps protect the gut lining and keeps things moving.

Without enough fiber, the mucosal layer thins, leaving us more vulnerable. Ashley noted that even people eating “healthy” can fall short if they’re not intentional.

## Prebiotics, Probiotics & Polyphenols

Probiotics are the beneficial bacteria. Prebiotics are the food that fuels them — think onions, garlic, and fermented foods like sauerkraut and kimchi. Polyphenols (from berries, turmeric, cinnamon) add another layer of support.

You can get these through food or quality supplements. Ashley emphasized that while supplements help, food sources build resilience over time.

## Water and Motility — The Basics We Often Miss

Water keeps motility smooth — the movement of food through your system. Dehydration slows everything down and can lead to reabsorbing waste. Ashley sees low water intake in most clients.

Aim for consistent hydration. It supports digestion, nutrient absorption, skin health, and brain function.

## Signs Your Gut Might Need Attention

Common indicators include:
* Gas and bloating after meals
* Diarrhea, constipation, or both
* Brain fog, anxiety, or mood shifts
* Skin issues like eczema or acne
* Feeling overly full from normal portions
* New or increasing food sensitivities

If several of these sound familiar, it’s worth looking closer.

### Practical Takeaways

* **Hit your fiber target** — Aim for 25–30g daily through whole foods. Add chia seeds or berries if you’re coming up short.
* **Support with pre- and probiotics** — Include onions, garlic, fermented foods, or a quality supplement.
* **Drink enough water** — Consistent hydration keeps motility smooth and helps everything function better.
* **Watch for imbalance signs** — Track bloating, bowel changes, brain fog, or skin issues as clues.
* **Consider a gentle reset** — Ashley’s 2-week protocol (clean eating + supportive herbs) can help identify triggers and reduce toxin load.
* **Test when needed** — A GI map can reveal pathogens, imbalances, and digestion markers for personalized guidance.

### Final Thoughts

Gut health isn’t about perfection. It’s about paying attention and giving your body what it consistently needs. Small, steady changes compound into better energy, clearer thinking, and more resilience.

I’m grateful for conversations like this that turn complex topics into doable steps. Thanks to Ashley for sharing her expertise so openly.
